Ames

Since 2020, I have been an active member of this lab. I graduated from TAMUCC in 2024 with a Bachelor’s in Biology; concentration in animals. As an undergraduate I completed two DIS’s and contributed to two LSAMP Scholar projects. Currently, I am continuing my projects; in doing so, I am obtaining post-baccalaureate work experience at the lab.  My research consists of amino acids and their nitrogen isotopic ratios in the eye lenses found in Roundnose Grenadier (Coryphenoides rupestris). As well as several projects focusing on the comparative analysis of amino acid profiles and nitrogen isotope patterns in zooplankton and associated fecal pellets.
